6.1.1. Find and prepare MR models with MrParse¶

MrParse identifies structural homologs, suitable for making MR search models, in PDB and AFDB (AlphaFold Data Base). Relevant structures are selected on the basis of sequence alignment with PHMMER software. PHMMER implements methods using probabilistic models called profile hidden Markov Models, and is considered to be one of the most sensitive sequence-comparison software.

Found structure homologs are then trimmed to maximising their resemblance to target sequence and increasing chances for success in Molecular Replacement. If reflection data set is provided (recommended), MrParse uses the Phaser software to calculate eLLG scores for MR models found [Oeffner, 2018]. MR models with higher eLLG are almost certainly better for Molecular Replacement than models with lower eLLG scores and should be tried first.

MR search models, prepared by MrParse task, may be used in Molecular Replacement tasks (Phaser-MR or Molrep) directly, or assembled in MR Ensembles before phasing, which may increase chances for success.
